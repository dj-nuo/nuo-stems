### Do I need to purchase a new license for NUO-STEMS 4?
Yes, NUO-STEMS 4 is a separate license. You can't use your NUO-STEMS 3 license for NUO-STEMS 4.

All users who purchased NUO-STEMS 3 **after 1 November 2025 will get a free upgrade** to NUO-STEMS 4.

**Patreon subscribers will get a free upgrade** to NUO-STEMS 4.
Just install NUO-STEMS 4 and your license will be activated automatically.

### I want to generate stems on my Gaming PC and then use them on my DJ Laptop. Can I do this?
Yes, you can use [Legacy / Export Mode](/docs/getting-started/legacy-traktor) to generate .stem.m4a files on your Gaming PC.
Then you can use [Traktor Utilities](/docs/traktor-utilities/migrate-stem-m4a-to-tp4) to migrate .stem.m4a files to linked stems on your DJ Laptop Traktor collection.
Using Traktor Utilities doesn't require paid license.

### I use only Traktor Pro 3, can I still use NUO-STEMS 4?
Yes, you can use NUO-STEMS 4 with Traktor Pro 3 by generating .stem.m4a files (aka "legacy" stems).
BUT, there are no ["Traktor Utilities"](/docs/v3/traktor-utilities/general/) similar to NUO-STEMS 3 which would allow you to transfer metadata between originals and stems.
Essentially, you need to treat .stem.m4a files as first class citizens - and get rid of original files (e.g. MP3/WAV/etc).

This is a deliberate decision - I want you to support Traktor team by upgrading to Traktor Pro 4. I strongly believe that 50$ (as of Black Friday deals) is a great value for what youâ€™re getting - itâ€™s an investment into future of Traktor.

You can read more about this in [Legacy / Export Mode](/docs/getting-started/legacy-traktor) section.

### Can I convert my existing .stem.m4a files (created in NUO-STEMS 3) to NUO-STEMS 4 native stems?
No. Because NUO-STEMS 4 now has a different decoding mechanism. This results in a different track length than NUO-STEMS 3 (which relied on additional silence added at the beginning of the track and offset from the original track)

NUO-STEMS 4 now has exactly the same track length as the original track.

### If I'm using 320 AAC CBR stems, is it still better to process using FLAC files vs high quality MP3? I keep most of my music in mp3 (but I convert them from FLAC myself using high quality conversion)
Depends on your "lossless religion" ðŸ˜…. I'd say mp3 is fine. I just keep my library original all ALAC, as it's easier to download them this way. 
And in case future algorithms will need lossless for some reason - I'm covered.
But of course, I have random mp3 and m4a tracks from SoundCloud that are completely lossy.

Another argument for FLAC/ALAC originals is how mp3 gets decoded into WAV/FLAC/ALAC. If you've ever heard about [hotcue offset issues when converting hotcues between Traktor/RB/Serato/etc](https://pestrela.github.io/dj_kb/dj_collection_converters/#what-is-the-26ms-shift-issue-when-converting-cuesloops-between-softwares) - this is what it's related to - mp3 decoding which impacts absolute track duration.

If you're using "natively linked" 320 CBR NUO-STEMS 4 stems though, it relies on what's inside your collection (in your case - .mp3 originals). So if you're not using FLAC/ALAC in your collection - you won't be able to convert from FLAC/ALAC to "natively linked" STEMS as well (due to the mp3 decoding issues described above).

### NUO-STEMS is "stuck" at "Checking License" or "License Check Failed"
There might be few potential causes:

1.  Your computer is not connected to the internet. NUO-STEMS requires internet connection on the startup to check the license.
2.  Your provider/system/WiFi-router blocks `https://nuo-stems.com` from being reachable.
    1.  Restart computer
    2.  **Restart WiFi router**
    3.  Launch NUO-STEMS
3.  Windows PowerShell is for some reason "broken" on your computer
    1.  Follow this guide to fix it:[link](https://support.slatedigital.com/hc/en-us/articles/20352080857363-Powershell-is-not-recognized)

If none of the above helps - try settings your DNS to Google DNS: 8.8.8.8 and 8.8.4.4. Instructions: [link](https://developers.google.com/speed/public-dns/docs/using)


### I don't have originals in my collection, I only use .stem.m4a files. Can I still use NUO-STEMS 4?
Yes, you have 2 options:
1. Just re-convert your existing .stem.m4a files to NUO-STEMS 4 .stem.m4a files. The only benefit you're getting here is improved STEMS quality thanks to the new algorithms.
2. Migrate to Traktor Pro 4 approach. Have both originals and "linked native" stems in your collection.
    1. :::info
        Currently, there is no straight-forward way to do this. But I will look into it in the future. So you might want to hold off from migration right now.
    2. If you still want to do this, you need to:
        1. Add originals to your collection.
        2. Analyze all originals in TP4 (no need to set hotcues).
        3. Close Traktor Pro 4.
        4. Open **NUO-STEMS 3** (not NUO-STEMS 4) and go to [Settings > Traktor Utilities](/docs/v3/traktor-utilities/general/). 
        5. Change "Transfer direction" to "STEMS to Original".
        6. Go to "Traktor Utilities" tab and click "Sync All".
        7. Now you have both originals and "linked native" stems in your collection synced. Originals shall have correct colors, hotcues, beatgrids, etc.
            1. :::warning 
                I would advice to check at least few originals to make sure that everything is synced correctly. 
        8. Delete all .stem.m4a files from your collection.
        9. Prooceed with generating new ["linked native"](/docs/getting-started/native-traktor-pro-4) stems from the originals using NUO-STEMS 4.
